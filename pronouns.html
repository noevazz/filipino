Pronouns

What is a pronoun?

A pronoun is a word that replaces a noun in a sentence.

A noun is a word that represents a person, place, thing, or idea. It is one of the basic building blocks of a sentence.
So, a noun can be a subject, but not all nouns are subjects. A noun becomes the subject when it plays that role in the sentence.

noun: apple (represents a thing).
pronoun: it (you can replace "apple" with the "it" pronoun).
subject: an noun and a pronoun can be subjects if they play that role in a sentence.

Pronouns in Filipino

Panhalip Panao are divided into three groups

  1. Ang group
  2. Ng group
  3. Sa group

Here is a table with the three groups:

Pronoun Group Singular/Plural Ang Group Ng Group Sa Group
First Person Singular ako ko akin
Plural (Inclusive) tayo natin atin
Plural (exclusive) kami namin amin
Second Person Singular ikaw/ka mo iyo
Plural kayo ninyo inyo
Third Person Singular siya niya kanya
Plural sila nila kanila

Kita

Kita is a unique pronoun, it is very hard to place it in one of the three groups


Simple sentence pattern

Subject / Focus / Topic +Copula +Predicate
personal pronoun "ay" adjective / noun / verb
Example:
ako ay matalino
I am smart
"Ay" is NOT a verb like "to be" in English. Instead, it is a linking word (sometimes called an inversion marker) used in formal or literary Filipino sentences to connect the subject and predicate, often giving the sentence a more poetic or archaic tone. It typically appears in sentences that invert the typical subject-predicate order.
It is usually omitted in common conversations.
