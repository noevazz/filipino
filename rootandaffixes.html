In filipino, roots (ugat) and affixes (panlapi) are essential parts of word formation.

A root word is the base form of a word that carries the core meaning. It can stand alone or be modified with affixes to create new words.

Affixes are added to root words to change their meaning or function.

TO REMEMBER:
A noun is a word that represents a person, place, thing, or idea. It is one of the basic building blocks of a sentence.

nouns can refer to tangible things (like bahay for "house") or abstract concepts (like talino for "intelligence")

So, a noun can be a subject, but not all nouns are subjects. A noun becomes the subject when it plays that role in the sentence.
  • noun: apple (represents a thing).
  • pronoun: it (you can replace "apple" with the "it" pronoun).
  • subject: an noun and a pronoun can be subjects if they play that role in a sentence.
