Filipino is just standardized tagalog.

Tagalog

The word "Tagalog" is believed to come from the term "taga-ilog", which roughly translates to "people of the river" or "river dwellers."

It is derived from the words "taga", meaning "from" or "people of," and "ilog", meaning "river."

Historically, the Tagalog people lived near the rivers in the region of Luzon in the Philippines..

Tagalog & Filipino

While Filipino and Tagalog are often used interchangeably, Filipino incorporates words from other Philippine languages and foreign languages, making it a more standardized version used for communication across the nation.

Tagalog, on the other hand, refers specifically to the language spoken by people from the Tagalog region in Luzon.
