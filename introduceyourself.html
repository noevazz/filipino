Introduce yourself

"Ako si NAME" is used when talking with familiar people (friends, same age, family, etc.).
Using "po" shows respect, use it if you are talking with a person of higher hierarchy (boss, police man, new people you met, elder people, etc.).
In Filipino a name cannot stand by its own, it has to have a "case marker".
CASE MARKER + NOUN
(It also applies to inanimate nouns)
In this example, "si" indicates that "NAME" is the subject or topic being referred to. It is used for singular, non-honorific, and personal names.
"Ay" is NOT a verb like "to be" in English. Instead, it is a linking word (sometimes called an inversion marker) used in formal or literary Filipino sentences to connect the subject and predicate, often giving the sentence a more poetic or archaic tone. It typically appears in sentences that invert the typical subject-predicate order.
It is usually omitted in common conversations.
