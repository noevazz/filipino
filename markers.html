Markers are words that indicates the role of a word in the sentece.

There are three main markers:

  • ang: ang, si, sina, ang mga
  • ng: ng, ni, nina, ng mga
  • sa: sa, kay, kina, sa mga
The Ang Marker

Ang is a subject marker used to indicate the focus or topic of a sentence.

It functions similarly to the definite article the in English but has a more grammatical role in structuring sentences.

  • Marks the subject of the sentence:

    Tumatahol ang aso / The dog is barking.

    Kumakain ang batang lalaki / The boy is eating.

    Ang marks "batang lalaki" ("the boy") as the subject of the sentence.

    Another example just to expand your vocabulary:

    Umiinom ang batang babae / The girl is drinking.

  • Used in questions to identify or describe something:

    Ano ang pangalan mo? / What is your name?.

    Ang marks "pangalan" ("name") as the noun that is the focus of the question.

  • Ang is not exlusive to people

    magkano ang ice cream? / How much is the ice cream?

    It can be used for things, places, ideas, animals, etc. What matters is that it marks the focused subject of the sentence ‚Äî the main thing being talked about.

Is ang like the in English?

Yes... and also not exactly üòÖ

  • Ang and the seem similar because they both come before nouns.
  • In English, the just makes a noun specific; it doesn't mark its role in the sentence.
  • Ang has a grammatical function, it marks the subject that is in focus.

Note: batang means kid

If you add it to lalaki / man or babae / woman then you get:

batang lalaki / boy

batang babae / girl

Ang Mga

While ang is used for singular nouns, ang mga is used for plural nouns.

Examples:

  • Kumakain ang batang mga lalaki / The boys are eating.
  • Umiinom ang mga batang babae / The girls are drinking.
  • Nagsusulat ang mga lalaki / The men are writing.
  • Nagbabasa ang mga batang babae / The girls are reading.
  • Lumalangoy ang mga babae / The women are swimming.
  • Tumatakbo ang mga batang lalaki / The boys are running.
Si/Sina

Si and Sina are Filipino markers used with proper nouns, especially people's names, they can also be used for titles, nicknames, and personified entities.

In Filipino a name cannot stand by its own, it has to have a marker (it also applies to inanimate nouns).
In the examples below, si/sina indicates the subject or topic being referred to. It is used for singular, non-honorific, and personal names.

  • Si: used for singular subjects

    Example:

    si Noe / Noe

    Maganda si Andrea / Andrea is beautiful

  • Sina: used for plural subjects

    Example:

    sina Noe, Andrea at Mauricio / Noe, Andrea and Mauricio

    The word at / and is always used with sina since we are calling multiple persons.

The Ng marker

Ng is pronounced as nang.

Ng has different meanings depending on the context. It can indicate possession ("of"), or it can mark the direct object in an active sentence.

  1. Ng as a marker of possession (like "of" in English)

    Libro ng estudyante / book of the student

    Another way to say it:

    Ang libro ng estudyante / The book of the student

    With "ang" to emphasize "book"

  2. Ng as a marker of the direct object (in active sentences)

    Umiinom ng kape ang babae / The woman is drinking coffee

    Here, "ng kape" marks "kape" (coffee) as the object of the action "umiinom" (drinking).

    Another example:

    Gusto ng lalaki ang ice cream / The man likes the ice cream.

    • Gusto ng lalaki marks the doer (but not the focus)
    • ang ice cream marks the focused object (what he likes)

Key takeaways:

  • "ng" + noun (possession) ‚Üí "Libro ng estudyante" (possession: the student's book).
  • Verb + "ng" + noun (direct object) ‚Üí "Umiinom ng kape ang babae" (action: drinking coffee).
Common confusion

Gusto ng lalaki ang ice cream / The man likes the ice cream.

  • The verb "gusto" is an object-focus verb.
  • "ang ice cream" is the object and also the focus of the sentence, it's what is being liked.
  • "ng lalaki" is the experiencer of the feeling like, but he is not the focus.
  • Because the verb is object-focus, the focus/object is marked with ang, and the doer/experiencer is marked with ng.

Umiinom ng kape ang babae / The woman is drinking coffee

  • The verb "umiinom" is an actor-focus verb
  • "ang babae" is the subject and also the focus of the sentence, she is the one doing the drinking.
  • "ng kape" is the object of the action, what is being drunk, but it is not the focus
  • Because the verb is actor-focus, the object is marked with ng, and the doer is marked with ang.

Learning if the verb is actor-focus or object-focus is one core skill when learning Filipino, it is key to knowing:

  • who is the doer
  • what is the focus
  • how to mark each part (with ang, ng, sa, etc.)
