Markers are words that indicates the role of a word in the sentece.

There are three main markers:

  • ang: ang, si, sina, ang mga
  • ng: ng, ni, nina, ng mga
  • sa: sa, kay, kina, sa mga
The Ang Marker

Ang is a subject marker used to indicate the focus or topic of a sentence.

It functions similarly to the definite article the in English but has a more grammatical role in structuring sentences.

  • Marks the subject of the sentence:

    Kumakain ang batang lalaki / The boy is eating.

    Ang marks "batang lalaki" ("the boy") as the subject of the sentence.

    Another example just to expand your vocabulary:

    Umiinom ang batang babae / The girl is drinking.

  • Used in questions to identify or describe something:

    Ano ang pangalan mo? / What is your name?.

    Ang marks "pangalan" ("name") as the noun that is the focus of the question.

Finally, you can also find ng as one word, it means "of" and it is pronounced as "nang".

ng is used as "genitive marker" which functions as "of" in english, example:

Libro ng estudyante

Which means Book of the student or the student's book.
