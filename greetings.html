Some people pronounce "kamusta" but this is wrong, I encourage you to pronounce "kumusta" but at the end of the day it's up to you.
Also keep in mind that "kumusta ka?" and "kumusta?", or even "kumusta kayo?" (plural) are recommended to use ONLY if you are familiar with the person (friends, same age, family, etc.)

po is a polite particle used to show respect, especially when speaking to someone older or of higher status.

Even though "kayo" means "you" (plural), you can use it here for both single person and more than one person. Using "kayo" to a single person shows even more respect.

"Kumusta po sila" (only plural) is used to show extreme respect.

Rin means too, also or either. Rin is commonly used after words ending in a vowel, Din is commonly used after words ending in a consonant but this rule is not strict.

Greeting people depending on the time of the day

Madaling Araw (Dawn – 12:00 AM to ~5:00 AM)
Informal: Magandang madaling araw! Formal: Magandang madaling araw po!
Umaga (Morning – ~5:00 AM to 11:59 AM)
Informal: Magandang umaga! Formal: Magandang umaga po!
Tanghali (Noon – 12:00 PM to ~1:00 PM)
Informal: Magandang tanghali! Formal: Magandang tanghali po!
(Some people just use "Magandang hapon" instead of "Magandang tanghali.")
Hapon (Afternoon – ~1:00 PM to 5:59 PM)
Informal: Magandang hapon! Formal: Magandang hapon po!
Gabi (Evening – ~6:00 PM to 11:59 PM)
Informal: Magandang gabi! Formal: Magandang gabi po!
Hatinggabi (Midnight – 12:00 AM)
Informal: Magandang hatinggabi! Formal: Magandang hatinggabi po!
(This is also rarely used. People usually just say “Magandang gabi” instead.)
