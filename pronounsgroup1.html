Pronouns Ang group

Person Filipino Pronoun English Translation
1st Person Singular Ako I
2nd Person Singular Ikaw/ka You
3rd Person Singular Siya He/She
1st Person Plural Kami We (exclusive)
1st Person Plural Tayo We (inclusive)
2nd Person Plural Kayo You (plural/singular - polite)
3rd Person Plural Sila They
"Kami" excludes the listener. E.g. We are going to the cinema (but not you, the listener).
"Tayo" includes the listener. E.g. We are going to the cinema (and you're coming with us).
In Filipino, "s" sounds like "sh" when followed by "y" or a "y" sound, as in "siya" (sha). Otherwise, it retains the regular "s" sound, as in "sila" (see-la).

Simple sentence pattern

Subject / Focus / Topic +Copula +Predicate
personal pronoun "ay" adjective / noun / verb
Example:
ako ay matalino
I am smart
"Ay" is NOT a verb like "to be" in English. Instead, it is a linking word (sometimes called an inversion marker) used in formal or literary Filipino sentences to connect the subject and predicate, often giving the sentence a more poetic or archaic tone. It typically appears in sentences that invert the typical subject-predicate order.
It is usually omitted in common conversations.
