Introduce yourself

Ako si Noe / I am Noe
"Ako si NAME" is used when talking with familiar people (friends, same age, family, etc.).

Another ways to say it:

Ang aking pangalan ay Noe / My name is Noe
Ako po ay si NAME / Ako po si NAME / I am NAME (formal)
Using "po" shows respect, use it if you are talking with a person of higher hierarchy (boss, police man, new people you met, elder people, etc.).

Ay is NOT a verb like "to be" in English. Instead, it is a linking word (sometimes called an inversion marker) used in formal or literary Filipino sentences to connect the subject and predicate, often giving the sentence a more poetic or archaic tone.

It typically appears in sentences that invert the typical subject-predicate order.

It is usually omitted in common conversations.

Ask someone's else name

Anong pangalan mo? / What is your name?

pangalan means name

The full form is Ano ang pangalan mo?, but people usually collapse the first two words into Anong

More questions

  • Singular familiar:

    Taga saan ka? / Where are you from?

  • Plural familiar:

    Taga saan kayo? / Where are you from?

  • Polite Singular/Plural:

    Taga saan po kayo? / Where are you from?

You can reply with this:

  • Singular familiar

    Taga Mexico ako / I am from Mexico

  • Singular polite

    Taga Mexico po ako / I am from Mexico

  • Plural familiar

    Taga Mexico kami / We are from Mexico

  • Plural polite

    Taga Mexico po kami / We are from Mexico

You can use taga to describe the "doer" of something.

  • The word salin means translate.

    Taga salin / Translator

  • The word masid means observe.

    Taga masid / Observer

  • The word linis means clean.

    Taga masid / Cleaner

There are specific words in tagalog for the occupations, e.g.

Tsuper / Driver

But taga is handy when you don't remember the word:

Taga maneho / Driver

The word maneho means drive

Finally, the word taga can be used in a joking manner or used in a not flattering way:

Joking:

  • Taga kain / Someone who eats a lot
  • Taga drink / Someone who drinks a lot
  • Taga kanta / Someone who sings a lot

Not flattering:

  • Taga mando / Someone that gives orders even when it's not his job
  • Taga sigaw / Someone who shout a lot
