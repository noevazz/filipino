The Articles Si and Ang

In Filipino, any noun used as the subject in a sentence is always preceded by an article (except in imperative statements).

An imperative sentence is a type of sentence that gives a command, instruction, request, or advice. It usually begins with the base form of a verb and often has an implied subject ("you").

Example:

  • Close the door. (command)
  • Please sit down. (polite request)
  • Don't touch that. (negative command)
  • Remember to call me. (advice)
Si and Siná

á is only to show you where to stress the word but the real word is just sina

The si article is used before the name of a person, sina is used before the names of two or more persons.

Examples:

  • Si Noe ay lalaki / Noe is a man
  • Sina Noe at Andrea ay matatalino / Noe and Andrea are good

You can use si instead of sina but then you need to repeat si for each person name:

  • Si Noe at si Andrea ay matatalino / Noe and Andrea

Adjectives form their plural by duplicating the first syllable, that's why in the example aboce we used matatalino:

  • Root word: talino (intelligence)
  • State: matalino (to be smart)
  • Plural: matatalino (to be smart)
Ang and Ang Mga

When the noun is the name of a thing or place we use ang and for plurar we use ang mga

The pronunciation of mga is manga.

  • Ang aso ay mabait / The dog is good
  • Ang aso at ang bata ay mabait / The dog and the child are good

In contrasts with sina, for ang mga is not used for listing, therefore you need to repeat ang or ang mga for the next nouns. Although is acceptable to delete the second ang before the second name.
