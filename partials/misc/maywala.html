May/Wala

In Filipino, may and wala are commonly used to express possession or existence.

  • May means "there is" or "has/have".

    May tubig sa mesa / There is water on the table
    May bolpen ang lalaki / The man has a pen

  • Wala is the opposite, it means "there is none" or "does not have".

    Wala akong pera / I don't have money

    The word walang is a shortened form of wala na may or more naturally, wala'ng, and is used before a noun:

    The word na is functioning as a linker or ligature, a very common feature in Filipino grammar.

    na is used to connect words, especially when the first word ends in a vowel and the second word is another word (like a noun or modifier). It's similar to how we use "of" or "-'s" in English, or how adjectives are linked to nouns.

    wala na may is not a standard expression anymore. It's more of a theoretical or old grammatical structure. In natural Filipino today, we just say walang.

    Walang kaning ang mga batang babae / The girls does not have rice
