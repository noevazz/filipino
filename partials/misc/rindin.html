How To Say "Also/Too"

rin and din both mean "also" or "too", and they are interchangeable in meaning, but their usage depends on the sound of the word that comes before them.

🤓 Basic rule (not recommended to learn):

  • Use rin after words that end in a vowel (a, e, i, o, u) or letters “w” and “y”.
  • Use din after words that end in a consonant except “w” and “y.”

👉 The rule aboce looks at how the word is written, not how it's pronounced.

⚠️ IMPORTANT ⚠️

In Filipino, a word ending in a vowel with a glottal stop (like "ka", "siya", or "Maria") is treated phonetically like a consonant, so we use "rin."

A glottal stop is a type of sound made by briefly closing the vocal cords, stopping the airflow, and then releasing it. It's the sound you hear in the middle of the English interjection:

"uh-oh" → the little pause between “uh” and “oh” is a glottal stop.

In phonetics, it's written as [ʔ] and in Tagalog, it's very common at the ends of words that appear to end in vowels.

In Filipino, many words that end in a vowel are actually pronounced with a glottal stop at the end, especially when spoken in isolation or stressed. So words like:

  • ka
  • siya
  • bata
  • Maria

are often pronounced like:

  • ka?
  • siya?
  • bata?
  • Maria?

That tiny pause/catch in your throat at the end makes the vowel feel “cut off,” and this is what makes the word phonetically act like it ends in a consonant — so we use "rin" instead of "din."

⭐ Phonetic/Modern Rule (Based on pronunciation/sound):

This rule is based on how the word actually sounds when spoken, especially considering glottal stops (which aren't written in standard Filipino spelling).

  • Use rin after words that end in vowel SOUNDS or n
  • Use din after words that end in consonant SOUNDS, including vowels with glottal stops.

👉 This rule matches how native speakers naturally choose "rin/din" in speech, and is more accurate in real life.

👀 Examples:

Gusto ko rin. / I like it too.
Si Tonyo rin. / Tonyo too.
Kumain ka din. / You ate too.
Uminom siya rin. / He/she drank too.
