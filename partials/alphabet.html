Alphabet

In the Philippines, the writing system and alphabet has evolved over time:

  • Abecedario: This was the Spanish-influenced alphabet used during the Spanish colonial period. It consisted of 32 letters, including Spanish-specific ones like "Ch," "Ll," and "Ñ."
  • Abakada: Introduced by Lope K. Santos in 1940, this was the native Filipino alphabet with 20 letters: A, B, K, D, E, G, H, I, L, M, N, Ng, O, P, R, S, T, U, W, and Y. It was designed to better represent the sounds of native Filipino languages.
  • Alpabetong Filipino: The current Filipino alphabet, adopted in 1987, has 28 letters, incorporating the original Abakada and adding the letters C, F, J, Ñ, Q, V, X, and Z to represent sounds from foreign words and indigenous languages.
