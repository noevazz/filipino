Verbs

Verbs are action words that change depending on:

  • Focus (who or what the sentence is about).
  • Aspect (whether the action is completed, ongoing, or not yet started).
  • Voice (like active or passive, tied closely to focus).
Unlike English, Filipino verbs don't change with subject pronouns (I, you, he, she), but they change with what you're emphasizing in the sentence (actor or object).

Focus

Focus is what part of the sentence is being emphasized, is it the doer (actor), the object, the location, the beneficiary, etc.?

Focus Type What's Emphasized Common Affixes Example
Actor-Focus The one doing the action um-, mag-, ma- Kumain si Ana. (Ana ate.)
Object-Focus The thing being acted on -in-, i-, -an Kinain ni Ana ang mansanas. (The apple was eaten by Ana.)
Location-Focus The place where the action happens -an Pinagluto ni Ana ang kusina. (Ana cooked in the kitchen.)
Benefactive-Focus The person for whom the action is done i-, ipag- Ipinagluto ni Ana si Ben. (Ana cooked for Ben.)
Instrumental-Focus Focus The tool used to do the action ipang- Ipinangsulat niya ang lapis. (He used the pencil to write.)

So focus changes what role in the sentence is highlighted, and the verb affix changes accordingly.

Aspect

This shows when the action happens. It's not quite like tense (past/present/future) in English but focuses on completion.

  • Completed (Perpektibo): the action is done

    kumain / ate

  • Incompleted (Imperpektibo): the action is ongoing

    kumakain / is eating

  • Contemplated (Kontemplatibo): the action is not yet started

    kakain / will eat

These forms depend on affixes + sometimes repetition of syllables.

VOICE

Voice in Filipino is tightly connected to Focus — it tells you if the sentence is in active voice or passive voice.

In other languages, like English:

  • Active: Ana ate the apple.
  • Passive: The apple was eaten by Ana.

In Filipino, this is handled not by reordering words, but by changing the verb focus.

Voice Filipino Equivalentd Focus Type
Active Voice The subject is the doer Actor-Focus
Passive Voicee The subject receives the action Object-Focus, Location-Focus, etc.

  • Active (Actor-Focus):

    Kumain si Ana ng mansanas.

    ➤ Ana is the focus/doer.

  • Passive (Object-Focus):

    Kinain ni Ana ang mansanas.

    ➤ The apple is the focus/receiver.

The action is the same (eating), but the focus (and thus the verb form) changes depending on who or what you want to highlight.
