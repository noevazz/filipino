Understanding the Filipino Particle "ay"

Ay is a grammatical particle used in Filipino to mark an inverted sentence structure, especially in formal or written language.

It separates the subject from the predicate and emphasizes the subject by placing it at the beginning of the sentence.

  • Predicate: The part of the sentence that tells us something about the subject, such as what they are doing, where they are from, or what they are. Predicate can contain multiple words, verbs, etc.
