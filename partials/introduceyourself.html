Introduce yourself

Ako si Noe / I am Noe
"Ako si NAME" is used when talking with familiar people (friends, same age, family, etc.).

Another ways to say it:

Ang aking pangalan ay Noe / My name is Noe
Ako po ay si NAME / Ako po si NAME / I am NAME (formal)
Using "po" shows respect, use it if you are talking with a person of higher hierarchy (boss, police man, new people you met, elder people, etc.).

Ay is NOT a verb like "to be" in English. Instead, it is a linking word (sometimes called an inversion marker) used in formal or literary Filipino sentences to connect the subject and predicate, often giving the sentence a more poetic or archaic tone.

It typically appears in sentences that invert the typical subject-predicate order.

It is usually omitted in common conversations.

Ask someone's else name

Anong pangalan mo? / What is your name?

pangalan means name

The full form is Ano ang pangalan mo?, but people usually collapse the first two words into Anong
