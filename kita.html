Kita

"Kita" is a contracted pronoun that combines "I" ako and "you" ikaw/ka into a single word.

It is used only when:.

  • The speaker (I) is the actor
  • The listener (you) is the object of the action.

So it's like saying:

"I [do something] to you."

✅ When to Use kita

Use kita when:

  • The speaker (I) is the actor
  • The listener (you) is the object of the verb
  • The verb is transitive and in actor-focus
📌 Examples:
Filipino English
Mahal kita. I love you.
Kita ko. I saw you.
Makikita kita bukas. I will see you tomorrow.
Hinahanap kita. I’m looking for you.
Narinig kita. I heard you.
Tatawagan kita. I will call you.
✖️ When not to use kita
  • When you are the subject/actor → use ikaw
  • When the verb is object-focus → use ka or ikaw

Examples:

  • Ikaw ang mahal ko. = You are the one I love.
  • Minahal kita. = I loved you. ✅
  • Minahal ka. = Someone else loved you. ❌ (Ambiguous)
🧠 Quick Tip: If you receive the action from me → use kita.
